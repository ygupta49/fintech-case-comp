1
Wearable opt-in
Customer connects Apple Watch, Fitbit, or Garmin via HealthKit or Health Connect during TD app setup. A plain-language consent screen explains exactly what is shared. Revocable in one tap anytime.
2
On-device signal processing
Heart rate, HRV, and skin conductance are processed locally on the phone. A lightweight on-device model computes a stress score (0–100). Raw biometric data never leaves the device — only the derived score is used during an active transfer session.
3
Friction Engine fusion
The transaction risk score (amount, recipient novelty, timing, account history) is fused with the biometric stress index. High transaction risk + elevated HR = longer hold, more explicit copy. Low transaction risk + elevated HR = gentle check-in only.
4
Contextual intervention
Copy adapts to the signal combination. Coercion scenario (very high HR + large new-contact transfer) surfaces the silent duress option. Anxiety scenario (moderate HR + new contact) shows a "take a breath" pause. Normal HR + flagged transaction = standard friction, no biometric mention at all.
5
Wearable haptic feedback
When the Friction Engine fires, the Apple Watch or Fitbit delivers a quiet haptic tap simultaneously. In a coercion scenario where someone is being watched, a private wrist tap is a signal the attacker can't see — the phone screen doesn't need to change visibly at all.
Raw data stays on your device
Heart rate, HRV, and skin conductance never leave your phone. Only a single derived stress score is passed to the Friction Engine, and only during an active transfer session — never stored or logged.
Explicit opt-in only
Biometric integration is off by default. It requires a separate consent from standard app permissions, with a plain-language explanation of every signal and how it is used. Off by default, always.
Never used to deny service
Biometric signals can only add friction or trigger a protective pause. They can never block a transaction, affect your credit, or be shared with any third party — including insurers or advertisers.
Revoke in one tap
Disconnecting wearable integration takes one tap in TD Settings → Privacy → Health data. All derived scores are purged immediately. The Friction Engine continues on transaction signals alone.
Duress gesture — coercion exception
Customers can pre-configure a double-press watch crown gesture that silently alerts TD and emergency services without changing what appears on screen — protecting them from a threat that's physically present.

Biometric integration uses Apple HealthKit and Google Health Connect APIs. TD never has direct access to the wearable device or its full health record.
